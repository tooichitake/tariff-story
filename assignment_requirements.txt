Project Brief: The Data Narrative Studio

Weighting: 40% (Total) | Format: Group (6-7 members) | Tooling: Tableau OR Streamlit + GitHub + SharePoint/Teams

1. The Challenge

You are a specialist data consultancy. Your task is to transform complex, multi-dimensional data into an interactive, persuasive narrative. This is not a "status dashboard"; it is a human-centered story designed to invoke emotion, convince with data, and drive a specific action.

Subject Intended Learning Outcomes (SILOs)

This project assesses your ability to:

SILO 1: Justify the selection and analysis of data as the basis for ‘data narratives’ for different stakeholders.
SILO 2: Apply a range of visualisation and narrative techniques to a variety of data types.
SILO 3: Justify the selection of narrative tools and techniques to illuminate critical aspects of problems.
SILO 4: Justify and communicate ‘data narratives’ to stakeholders from a range of industries and contexts drawing on relevant data patterns and analyses.
2. Technical & Narrative Constraints

The "Rich Data" Requirement

Real-World & Recent: No synthetic data. Avoid "Blacklisted" sets (e.g., Titanic, Superstore, Iris). Data must be collected from 2024 onwards.
Dimensions: Must include Temporal (datetime) or Spatial (names, lat/long, ISO) variables.
Scale: You must design at least 4 purposeful visuals (minimum).
Enrichment (The DI/HD Path): High-distinction projects go beyond a single CSV by joining/enriching disparate datasets to reveal deeper "So What" insights.
Human-Centered Design (HCD)

User Persona & Stories: Define a specific audience and "User Stories" to justify every visual choice. Must have acceptance criteria and definition of done.
Visual Principles: E.g. Apply Gestalt principles, pre-attentive attributes, and cognitive load optimization.
Accessibility: E.g. Dashboards must demonstrate high colour contrast and intuitive affordance.
The "Advanced" Features

You must implement at least three of the following (or tool-specific equivalents):

Context-Aware Filtering: Selections in one visual update narrative text or other visuals dynamically (e.g., Streamlit callbacks or Tableau Parameter Actions).
Visual Tooltips/Modals: Hover effects revealing deeper insights (e.g., mini-charts within tooltips or Viz-in-Tooltip).
Narrative Scrollytelling: Using layout components to guide the user through a structured flow.
What-If Parameterization: Sliders/inputs allowing users to model alternative future scenarios.
Narrative Arc Options

Groups must select and justify one of the following narrative structures:

What ➡ So What ➡ What Next: The classic executive efficiency arc.
The Martini Glass: Author-driven narrative leads into a user-driven "sandbox" exploration.
The Detective: Starting with a data anomaly (the "crime") and uncovering the "culprit" (the variable).
The Sparkline: Highlighting the gap between "what is" (current data) and "what could be" (the goal).
