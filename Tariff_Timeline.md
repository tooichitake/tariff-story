# Trump Tariff War: Complete Timeline
## January 2025 — April 2026

**Project Context**: AT3 Data Narrative Studio — "The Tariff Tax: Who Pays?"
**Stakeholder**: US President
**Narrative**: Even if tariff inflation is a one-time price shift (as the Fed's base case suggests), the burden falls disproportionately on low-income households — 3.6% of income for the bottom decile vs 1.1% for the top.

- Bottom income decile bears 3.6% of income in tariff costs vs top decile 1.1% (Yale Budget Lab)

- TPC estimates avg household burden ~$2,100 in 2026; bottom quintile federal tax rate rises 1.9pp vs top 1% rises 0.7pp

## Key Distributional Data Points (for "Who Pays" narrative)

| Metric | Bottom 20% | Middle 20% | Top 20% | Top 1% | Source |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------|--------|--------|
| Avg tax increase (2026) | $400 | $1,610 | $7,330 | ~$40,000 | TPC |
| Federal tax rate increase | +1.9pp | +1.6pp | +1.3pp | +0.7pp | TPC |
| Share of income lost (short-run) | -3.6% | ~-2% | ~-1.5% | -1.1% | Yale |
| Most affected goods | Apparel, shoes, food | Electronics, furniture | Vehicles, luxury goods | Financial assets | Multiple |

**The critical insight**: The dollar amounts are higher for wealthy households, but the *share of income* is much higher for poor households. A $400 increase for a family earning $25,000/year is a fundamentally different burden than $40,000 for a family earning $5,000,000/year.
